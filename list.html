A review of 3 of my favourite Tv shows/series' :

Arcane

Arcane is a stunning animated series that brings the League of Legends universe to life with breathtaking visuals and a gripping story. Set in Piltover and Zaun, it explores power, inequality, and family through the complex relationship of sisters Vi and Jinx. The animation is groundbreaking, and the soundtrack enhances the emotional depth. Whether you're a fan of the game or not, Arcane is a must-watch, setting a new standard for animated storytelling.

Rating: 10/10

Drive

Drive is a stylish and intense neo-noir thriller that blends raw emotion with bursts of visceral action. Ryan Gosling delivers a mesmerizing performance as a mysterious stunt driver who becomes entangled in a dangerous heist. Nicolas Winding Refn’s direction is masterful, with deliberate pacing, striking visuals, and a hauntingly atmospheric synth soundtrack. The film’s minimalist dialogue and electrifying sequences create a unique, almost hypnotic experience. Drive is a modern classic that balances artful storytelling with moments of gripping tension.

Rating: 9/10


Lookback (2024) by Kiyotaka Oshiyama:

Lookback (2024), directed by Kiyotaka Oshiyama, is a beautifully crafted animated film that blends powerful storytelling with stunning visuals. The film follows the journey of a young girl, who revisits her past to confront a traumatic event. Oshiyama’s unique animation style enhances the emotional depth of the narrative, with expressive character designs and soft, fluid motion that perfectly capture the introspective tone of the story. The film explores themes of memory, guilt, and redemption, with a focus on the healing process through self-reflection. The minimalist dialogue and atmospheric score deepen the film’s emotional impact, making it a poignant exploration of personal growth and closure. Lookback is a visually captivating, emotionally resonant film that lingers in the viewer's mind long after the credits roll.

Rating: 9/10
